# No heat in Unit 2A (January)

**Case ID:** broken-heater
**Score:** 9/10

## Incoming Situation

Maria Santos (Unit 2A) reports her heater stopped working. She says her apartment is freezing and she has a 6-month-old baby. It's January and the outside temperature is 28°F.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Maria Santos:**
> Maria, I'm treating this as an emergency — a baby in a cold apartment in January is a serious safety concern. I'm calling maintenance right now to get someone out to you as fast as possible. In the meantime, please bundle Sofia up with extra blankets and layers, and if you have a safe space away from the baby (like a bathroom) you can run hot water to warm a small area temporarily. Do NOT use the oven or stovetop for heat. I'll have an update for you within minutes.

**← Maria Santos replies:**
> Thank you so much for taking this seriously. Sofia is bundled up but the apartment is already down to about 55 degrees and dropping. I'm really scared for her. Do you know if maintenance has a portable heater they could bring? I don't think I can stay here tonight if this isn't fixed.

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to Joe's Maintenance:**
> Joe, EMERGENCY — Unit 2A has no heat. There is a 6-month-old baby in the unit, apartment is at 55°F and dropping, outside is 28°F. This is a habitability emergency. I need you out there ASAP. Do you have portable electric heaters you can bring as a temporary measure in case the repair takes time? Please call me back immediately with your ETA.

**← Joe's Maintenance replies:**
> I'll treat this as priority and head over within 2 hours. Yes, I have portable electric heaters I can bring as a backup. If it's the furnace, the part might need to be ordered and take 1-2 days, but the portable heaters will keep things safe in the meantime.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Maria Santos:**
> Maria, maintenance is on the way — they'll be there within 2 hours and they're bringing portable electric heaters as a backup in case the main repair takes longer. I want you to know that providing heat is a legal obligation and I'm not going to let you and Sofia spend a night in a freezing apartment. If for any reason the repair and portable heaters aren't sufficient, I'll arrange a hotel for tonight at the building's expense. You and Sofia's safety is the priority here.

**← Maria Santos replies:**
> Oh thank you, that's such a relief. The portable heaters would help a lot. I'll wait for maintenance. Thank you for thinking about a hotel backup too — I was really worried about tonight. You're being so helpful.

### Message 4

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, urgent situation in Unit 2A — heater is out, apartment at 55°F and dropping. Maria Santos has a 6-month-old baby in the unit, outside temp is 28°F. This is a habitability emergency with legal implications. I've dispatched Joe's Maintenance on emergency priority (ETA 2 hours), and he's bringing portable heaters as backup. I've also told Maria we'll cover a hotel tonight if the repair and heaters aren't sufficient — I wanted you to know before authorizing that spend. Heating failure in winter with a baby is our highest liability exposure. Please confirm you're okay with the hotel as a contingency.

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> Absolutely, do whatever it takes. Authorize the hotel if needed — I don't want any liability exposure here. A baby with no heat in January is a nightmare scenario. Keep me posted on what the repair looks like and the total cost. Thanks for jumping on this immediately.

## Agent Case Notes

## Case Status

## Judgment

Excellent emergency handling. The agent immediately recognized the severity — baby, freezing temps, legal habitability obligation — and acted accordingly. Communication with Maria was empathetic and practical, maintenance was dispatched urgently with a backup plan, and the owner was notified with clear framing of the liability exposure. The hotel contingency was proactively offered. Nearly textbook property management for this scenario.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM would have handled this almost identically. They might have additionally called a dedicated HVAC specialist instead of general maintenance if one was available, and might have checked whether the building has spare portable heaters on-site. They also would have documented the temperature readings as evidence for any potential legal issues.

- Call maintenance immediately for emergency service
