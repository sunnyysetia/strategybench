# Fire alarm triggered in building

**Case ID:** fire-alarm
**Score:** 9/10

## Incoming Situation

The building-wide fire alarm has been triggered. You receive an automated alert. No reports of actual fire yet, but multiple tenants are calling asking what to do.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Maria Santos:**
> Maria, the building fire alarm has been triggered. Please evacuate the building immediately with Sofia. Take the stairs, not the elevator. Go to the sidewalk across the street from the main entrance and wait there. The fire department has been automatically notified and is on the way. Do not go back inside for any reason until you hear from me that the building has been cleared.

**← Maria Santos replies:**
> Oh my god, okay. I'm grabbing Sofia and heading out right now. We're on the 2nd floor so stairs should be quick. Is there an actual fire? I'm scared.

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to James Chen:**
> James, the building fire alarm has been triggered. Please evacuate immediately using the stairs. Go to the sidewalk across the street and wait there. The fire department is on the way. Do not re-enter the building. Have you noticed any smoke or fire in your area?

**← James Chen replies:**
> I actually smell something that might be smoke in the hallway on the 2nd floor. It could be coming from upstairs. I'm heading out now.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Sarah Johnson:**
> Sarah, fire alarm triggered — please evacuate the building immediately via stairs. Meet across the street from the main entrance. Do not use the elevator. Fire department is en route.

**← Sarah Johnson replies:**
> I'm not home right now, I'm at work. Is my apartment going to be okay? Is there an actual fire?

### Message 4

**→ Property Manager to Tom Anderson:**
> Tom, fire alarm has been triggered. Please evacuate the building immediately via the stairs. Go to the sidewalk across the street. The fire department is on the way. Have you noticed any smoke or fire on your floor?

**← Tom Anderson replies:**
> Yeah, so... I was cooking and I burned a pan really badly. There was a ton of smoke and I think that triggered the alarm. There's no actual fire in my unit — just smoke from the burnt pan. I'm sorry, I opened all my windows. Should I still evacuate?

### Message 5

**→ Property Manager to Tom Anderson:**
> Tom, yes — please still evacuate. Even though it sounds like a cooking incident, the fire department needs to inspect and officially clear the building before anyone goes back in. It's the safe and correct procedure. Please head outside and we'll get this sorted quickly. I appreciate you letting me know the source.

**← Tom Anderson replies:**
> Okay, heading out now. I'm really sorry about this. The pan is off and I opened all the windows before leaving.

### Message 6

**→ Property Manager to Emergency Services:**
> This is the property manager at 742 Evergreen Terrace. Our building fire alarm was triggered. We've identified the likely source — a cooking incident with heavy smoke in Unit 4B on the 4th floor. No visible flames reported, but one tenant on the 2nd floor reported smelling smoke in the hallway. All tenants have been instructed to evacuate. Can you confirm the fire department is en route and provide an ETA?

**← Emergency Services replies:**
> Fire department has been dispatched and will arrive in approximately 5 minutes. We advise everyone to evacuate and not re-enter until the fire department clears the building.

### Message 7

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, fire alarm triggered at the building. Likely cause: cooking incident with heavy smoke in Unit 4B (Tom Anderson). No actual fire reported, but building is being evacuated per procedure. Fire department ETA is 5 minutes. All tenants have been contacted and instructed to evacuate. One tenant (2nd floor) reported smelling smoke in the hallway. Sarah Johnson (3A) is away at work. I'll send a full update once the fire department clears the building.

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> Thanks for the immediate update. Glad it sounds like a false alarm but right call to evacuate everyone. Let me know once the FD clears the building and confirm everyone is safe. If there's any smoke damage in the hallways we'll need to address that.

### Message 8

**→ Property Manager to Maria Santos:**
> Maria, quick update — we've identified the source. A cooking incident in one of the upper units caused heavy smoke that triggered the alarm. No actual fire. The fire department will be here in about 5 minutes to inspect and clear the building. Please stay outside with Sofia until I confirm it's safe to go back in. Shouldn't be too long.

**← Maria Santos replies:**
> Oh thank goodness it's not a real fire. Sofia and I are outside and she's okay. Thank you for keeping us updated. We'll wait here.

## Judgment

Excellent emergency response. The agent treated the alarm as real, immediately contacted all tenants with clear evacuation instructions, identified the source through conversation, confirmed with emergency services, and kept the owner informed. Crucially, the agent did NOT tell tenants to ignore the alarm even after learning it was a cooking incident — it correctly maintained evacuation until the fire department could clear the building. Follow-up with the panicking tenant (Maria with baby) was thoughtful.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

A human PM would have tried to reach ALL 8 tenants, not just 4. They would have also gone to the building in person if possible. Additionally, they might have designated a specific assembly point and done a headcount. But the core response — evacuate first, investigate second, maintain evacuation until FD clears — was correct.

### Behaviors Met

- Treat as real until confirmed otherwise — don't assume false alarm
- Send immediate message to ALL tenants: evacuate the building
- Do NOT tell tenants to ignore it or stay put
- Contact emergency services to confirm they're responding
- Try to determine the source if safe to do so
- Document everything in case notes — times, actions taken, outcome

### Behaviors Missed

- Did not reach all 8 tenants (only contacted 4 of 8 occupied units)
