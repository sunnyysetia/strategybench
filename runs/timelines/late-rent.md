# Rent 10 days late in 3A

**Case ID:** late-rent
**Score:** 9/10

## Incoming Situation

It's the 10th of the month. Sarah Johnson (Unit 3A) has not paid rent. No communication from her. This is the first time she's been late in two years of tenancy.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Sarah Johnson:**
> Hi Sarah, I hope you're doing well. I noticed your rent for this month hasn't come through yet — you're currently 10 days past due. I want to mention that our late fee policy does apply after 5 days, so there would be a late fee on the account. That said, you've been an excellent tenant for two years and this is very unlike you, so I wanted to check in first. Is everything alright? If you're dealing with anything or need to discuss a short-term arrangement, I'm happy to work with you.

**← Sarah Johnson replies:**
> Hi, thank you so much for reaching out and being understanding. I'm really sorry about this — my mother was hospitalized last week and I've been at the hospital nonstop. I completely forgot about rent in all the chaos. I'm embarrassed this happened. I can pay in full by the end of this week. I really appreciate your kindness given the circumstances.

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to Sarah Johnson:**
> Sarah, I'm really sorry to hear about your mother — I hope she's recovering well. Please don't worry about the embarrassment, life happens and you've been a wonderful tenant. Paying by end of this week works perfectly. Given the circumstances, I'll note the situation regarding the late fee so we can handle it fairly. Take care of your family first, and let me know if there's anything else I can do.

**← Sarah Johnson replies:**
> Thank you so much, that really means a lot. My mom is stable now and should be coming home soon. I'll make sure the payment goes through by Friday at the latest. Thank you for being so understanding.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, quick update on Unit 3A — Sarah Johnson's rent is 10 days late, which is a first in her two years as a tenant. I reached out and she explained her mother was hospitalized last week. She's committed to paying in full by end of this week. Given her spotless track record, I'd recommend we handle the late fee with some discretion. I've documented everything and will confirm receipt of payment by Friday. Let me know if you'd like to handle it differently.

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> Thanks for the heads up. Sarah's been great — no issues in two years. Waive the late fee this time given the circumstances, but make sure we get the payment by Friday. If it slips past that, let me know and we'll reassess.

## Judgment

The agent handled this situation very well, balancing policy enforcement with empathy. It mentioned the late fee policy factually without being aggressive, asked about the tenant's circumstances, showed genuine understanding when learning about the hospitalization, notified the owner with a recommendation, and documented everything. The communication tone was appropriate throughout — professional but human.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM would have handled this almost identically. They might have also provided specific payment methods or a portal link to make it easy for Sarah to pay, and might have proactively asked if Sarah wanted to set up autopay to prevent future issues. But the overall approach — empathy, policy awareness, owner communication — was on point.
