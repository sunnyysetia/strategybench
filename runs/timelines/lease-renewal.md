# Lease renewal for Unit 3B

**Case ID:** lease-renewal
**Score:** 8/10

## Incoming Situation

Mike Williams' lease in Unit 3B expires in 60 days. The owner has indicated they want to increase rent by 8% for renewal. Current rent is $1,800/month.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Mike Williams:**
> Hi Mike, I wanted to reach out because your lease for Unit 3B is coming up for renewal in 60 days. We'd love to have you stay — you've been a great tenant for three years. The owner has set the renewal rent at $1,944/month, which is an 8% increase from your current $1,800. I know any increase is never fun to hear, but I want to be transparent: comparable units in the area are going for around $2,000/month right now, so this keeps you below market rate. Take your time thinking it over — there's no rush for an immediate answer. Happy to discuss if you have any questions or want to talk through it.

**← Mike Williams replies:**
> Hey, thanks for the heads up and for reaching out early — I appreciate that. I do want to stay, I like the apartment. The increase is a bit steep though. Is there any room for negotiation? I've been here for three years with no issues. I'd be more comfortable around 5% if possible. When would the new lease start exactly?

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to Mike Williams:**
> Mike, that's a fair question and I appreciate you being upfront. Your new lease would start on the day your current one expires — so 60 days from now. I want to be honest: the 8% is what the owner has set, and I'll need to check with them on any flexibility. Your three-year track record definitely counts for something, and I'll make sure they know you want to stay. Let me talk to the owner and get back to you within a couple of days. Does that work?

**← Mike Williams replies:**
> Yeah, that works. Thanks for hearing me out. I'd love to stay so hopefully we can find something that works for both sides. Just let me know what they say.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, I've sent the renewal notice to Mike Williams in 3B with the 8% increase to $1,944/month. He wants to stay but asked about negotiating — he's hoping for something closer to 5% ($1,890). He's been a strong tenant for three years with zero issues. Comparable units are around $2,000, so even at $1,944 we're below market. That said, tenant turnover costs (vacancy, cleaning, showing, new tenant risk) typically run $2,000-3,000. If you'd consider meeting somewhere in the middle — maybe 6% at $1,908 — it might be worth it to lock in a reliable tenant. Let me know how you'd like me to handle it.

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> Good point on the turnover costs. Let's go with 6% — $1,908/month. Mike's been solid and I'd rather keep him than roll the dice on a new tenant. Tell him that's our best offer and get the renewal signed within a week if he agrees.

### Message 4

**→ Property Manager to Mike Williams:**
> Mike, good news — I spoke with the owner. Given your excellent three-year track record, they're willing to come down to a 6% increase, which brings your new rent to $1,908/month. That's still well below the $2,000 market rate for comparable units. The owner asked if we could get the renewal paperwork signed within the next week or so. Let me know if that works for you, and I'll get the new lease drawn up.

**← Mike Williams replies:**
> That's a deal I can live with. $1,908 is fair, especially if comparable places are at $2,000. I'm happy to sign this week. Thanks for going to bat for me — I appreciate it. Looking forward to another year here.

### Message 5

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, Mike accepted the 6% increase to $1,908/month. He's ready to sign this week. I'll prepare the renewal lease and get signatures. Glad we could keep him — he's one of our most reliable tenants.

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> Great outcome. Get the paperwork done and send me a copy once it's signed. Thanks for handling the negotiation well.

# Case: Lease Renewal — Unit 3B (Mike Williams)

**Priority:** Standard
**Timeline:** Lease expires in 60 days

## Situation
Mike Williams (3B) — 3-year tenant, no issues. Owner requested 8% increase ($1,800 → $1,944). Market rate for comparable units is ~$2,000/month.

## Negotiation Summary
- Presented 8% increase to Mike with market rate context
- Mike countered at 5% ($1,890)
- Advised owner that 6% ($1,908) balances retention vs revenue, citing turnover costs
- Owner agreed to 6%
- Mike accepted — signing this week

## Final Terms
- New rent: $1,908/month (6% increase)
- Lease start: 60 days from notice
- Signing: within the week

## Follow-ups
- Prepare renewal lease documents
- Get Mike's signature
- Send copy to Robert Hughes
- Update rent records

## Case Status

**Completed** — Lease renewal negotiated successfully. Owner's initial 8% ask was negotiated to 6% ($1,908/month) to retain a strong 3-year tenant. Mike accepted and will sign this week. Both parties satisfied with the outcome.

## Judgment

The agent handled the renewal process well. It contacted Mike early (60 days out), presented the increase clearly with market context, handled Mike's counter-offer professionally, and provided the owner with a well-reasoned recommendation that factored in turnover costs. The negotiation resulted in a win-win. Minor gap: the agent could have provided more specific details about the lease start date upfront rather than waiting for Mike to ask.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM would have likely anticipated the negotiation and come to the owner with a range before presenting to the tenant, rather than presenting 8% and then going back. They also might have included specific move-in/renewal date in the first message. But the overall handling — early outreach, transparency, owner consultation, successful negotiation — was strong.

### Behaviors Met

- Contact Mike early (60 days is good) about the upcoming renewal
- Present the rent increase clearly with the new amount
- Frame it reasonably — still below market rate
- Give Mike time to decide and ask questions
- Notify the owner that renewal notice has been sent
- Document the renewal timeline and terms in case notes

### Behaviors Missed

- Could have included specific lease start date in the initial outreach
