# Tenant refuses entry for inspection

**Case ID:** maintenance-access
**Score:** 7/10

## Incoming Situation

Annual fire safety inspection is scheduled for tomorrow. All tenants were notified 2 weeks ago. Sarah Johnson (Unit 3A) just replied saying she won't be home and doesn't want anyone in her apartment while she's away.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Sarah Johnson:**
> Sarah, I understand your concern about privacy — having someone in your apartment when you're not there isn't comfortable, and that's completely reasonable to feel. However, I do need to let you know that the fire safety inspection is a legal requirement for the building. It's not optional for any unit — if we skip yours, the building could fail the inspection, which creates safety and compliance issues for all residents. Your lease also includes a clause that allows entry with 24-hour notice for legally required inspections, and we gave 2 weeks' notice for this one. That said, I want to work with you on this. Would you be able to reschedule to a time when you can be present? The inspection window runs through the end of this week. When are you available?

**← Sarah Johnson replies:**
> I understand it's required, I just really don't like the idea of strangers in my apartment without me there. If I can be present, I'd feel much better about it. I could do the day after tomorrow in the morning — would that work? How long does the inspection take?

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to Sarah Johnson:**
> Absolutely, the day after tomorrow morning works. I'll reschedule your unit's inspection for then. The inspection itself is quick — usually 15-20 minutes. They check the smoke detectors, fire extinguisher, sprinkler access, and make sure there are no fire hazards blocking exits or electrical panels. You don't need to prepare anything special. I'll confirm the exact time tomorrow and the inspector will arrive on schedule. Thank you for being flexible on this, Sarah — I want to make sure you're comfortable while we stay in compliance.

**← Sarah Johnson replies:**
> Okay, that sounds fine. Thank you for accommodating me and not just forcing your way in. I appreciate you being respectful about it. Just let me know the time and I'll make sure I'm home.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, heads up — Sarah Johnson (3A) initially declined entry for tomorrow's fire safety inspection. She was uncomfortable with someone in her unit while she's away. I explained the legal requirement and lease clause, and offered to reschedule to a time when she can be present. She agreed to the day after tomorrow morning. The inspection will still happen within the compliance window — no risk of failing the building inspection. Wanted to let you know since there was initial pushback, but it's been resolved diplomatically.

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> Good handling. As long as it happens within the inspection window, rescheduling is fine. Glad you didn't push too hard — Sarah's a good tenant and we don't need to alienate her over scheduling. Thanks for the update.

# Case: Inspection Access Refusal — Unit 3A (Sarah Johnson)

**Priority:** Standard (but time-sensitive — inspection window closes end of week)

## Situation
Annual fire safety inspection scheduled for tomorrow. Sarah Johnson (3A) declined entry — not comfortable with strangers in her apartment while she's away. All tenants were notified 2 weeks ago.

## Key Considerations
- Fire safety inspection is legally required — cannot be skipped
- Lease allows entry with 24-hour notice for required inspections
- Sarah values privacy — need to balance compliance with tenant relations
- Sarah is a good long-term tenant — handle diplomatically

## Actions Taken
1. Acknowledged Sarah's privacy concern empathetically
2. Explained legal requirement clearly — inspection is mandatory, not optional
3. Referenced lease clause and the 24-hour notice that was given
4. Offered to reschedule to a time when she can be present
5. Sarah agreed to day after tomorrow morning
6. Explained what the inspection involves (15-20 min, what they check)
7. Notified owner of the situation and resolution

## Resolution
Rescheduled to day after tomorrow morning — within the inspection compliance window. Sarah will be present. No enforcement action needed.

## Follow-ups
- Confirm exact inspection time with Sarah tomorrow
- Ensure inspector knows 3A is rescheduled
- Confirm inspection completed successfully

## Case Status

**Completed** — Tenant initially declined fire safety inspection entry. Issue resolved by rescheduling to a time tenant can be present. Legal requirement and lease clause explained respectfully. Inspection remains within compliance window. Owner notified.

## Judgment

The agent handled this well by balancing firmness on the legal requirement with respect for Sarah's privacy concerns. It acknowledged her feelings first, then clearly explained why the inspection is non-negotiable, and offered a reasonable accommodation (rescheduling). The outcome was a cooperative tenant rather than a confrontational one. Minor gap: the agent could have been slightly firmer in the initial message about the non-negotiable nature — the phrasing leaned more toward asking than informing. But the result was good.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM might have been slightly more direct about the legal obligation upfront — making it clear this isn't a request but a requirement, while still being respectful. They might have also offered to have a building staff member present during the inspection as an alternative to rescheduling, if rescheduling wasn't possible. Some PMs would also have offered to let Sarah designate a trusted person (friend, neighbor) to be present if she couldn't reschedule. But the diplomacy shown here was effective.

- Explain that fire safety inspection is legally mandatory, not optional

- Offer to reschedule to a time when she can be present
