# Move-out inspection reveals damage

**Case ID:** move-out-damage
**Score:** 9/10

## Incoming Situation

Lisa Park (Unit 4A) has moved out. During the inspection you find: large stain on the living room carpet, two fist-sized holes in the bedroom wall, and a broken bathroom cabinet door. Normal wear items (minor paint scuffs, slightly worn carpet in hallways) are fine.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Joe's Maintenance:**
> Hi Joe, I just completed the move-out inspection for Unit 4A. I found three items of damage beyond normal wear and tear that need repair: 1) Large stain on the living room carpet — looks like a dark liquid, maybe wine or coffee. Carpet was installed 2 years ago. 2) Two fist-sized holes in the bedroom wall — these will need drywall patching, texture matching, and paint. 3) Broken bathroom cabinet door — door is off the hinge and cracked, needs replacement. Can you give me itemized repair estimates for each? I need them for the deposit deduction letter.

**← Joe's Maintenance replies:**
> Sure thing. Here are my estimates: wall hole patches — $75-100 each, so $150-200 for both, including texture matching and paint. Carpet stain — I can try treatment first ($50), but if that doesn't work, partial replacement would be $300-400 depending on the area. Cabinet door replacement — about $150 for the door and installation. So you're looking at $350 on the low end to $750 on the high end depending on the carpet. I can do all the work within a week.

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to Joe's Maintenance:**
> Thanks Joe. Let's plan for the carpet treatment first at $50 — if that doesn't remove the stain, we'll go with partial replacement and I'll update the deduction letter. Go ahead and schedule the wall patches ($200) and cabinet door ($150) — those are definite. Can you start this week? I want the unit ready for showing as soon as possible.

**← Joe's Maintenance replies:**
> I can start Thursday. I'll do the carpet treatment first, then the wall patches and cabinet door. If the stain doesn't come out, I'll let you know and we can decide on replacement. Should have everything done by Friday or Monday at the latest.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Lisa Park:**
> Lisa, I've completed the move-out inspection of Unit 4A. ## Judgment

Excellent handling of the move-out process. The agent properly distinguished between normal wear (no charge) and actual damage, got itemized estimates before sending the deduction notice, provided a clear written breakdown to Lisa, handled her dispute fairly by agreeing to check move-in records, and committed to providing photos and receipts. The agent was transparent about the legal timeline and kept the owner informed. Nearly textbook deposit handling.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM would have taken photos during the inspection for documentation (the agent didn't mention taking photos, just reviewing move-in photos). They also would have ideally had the move-in inspection photos ready before sending the deduction notice, rather than needing to check after the tenant disputed. Having both move-in and move-out photos side-by-side strengthens the landlord's position. But the process — itemization, distinguishing wear from damage, fair dispute handling, legal timeline awareness — was professional.

### Behaviors Met
