# Noise complaint between neighbors

**Case ID:** noise-complaint
**Score:** 9/10

## Incoming Situation

Lisa Park (Unit 4A) sends an angry message: 'I can't take it anymore. Unit 4B has been playing loud music past midnight every night this week. I have work at 6am and I'm losing my mind.'

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Lisa Park:**
> Lisa, I hear you — losing sleep night after night when you have early shifts is completely unacceptable, and I'm sorry you've been dealing with this. I'm going to contact Tom in 4B right now about the noise. Our building has quiet hours from 10pm to 8am, and loud music past midnight is a clear violation. I'll get back to you today with what he says and what steps we're taking. In the meantime, thank you for reporting this — it's exactly the right thing to do.

**← Lisa Park replies:**
> Thank you for actually taking this seriously. I knocked on his door twice this week and nobody answered. I'm a nurse and I literally cannot function on no sleep. I just need this to stop. I don't want to be a difficult tenant but this has been every single night.

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to Tom Anderson:**
> Hi Tom, I need to talk to you about a noise complaint from your neighbor in 4A. They've reported loud music going past midnight multiple nights this week. I want to be upfront — our building's quiet hours are 10pm to 8am, and this is something we need to address. I'm not here to make accusations, but your neighbor works early morning shifts and the noise is significantly affecting them. Can you tell me what's been going on, and can we find a solution here?

**← Tom Anderson replies:**
> Oh man, I'm really sorry about that. I got a new sound system recently and I've had some friends over a few nights. I genuinely didn't realize how much the sound carries through the walls. I feel bad — I wasn't trying to be inconsiderate. I'll absolutely keep it down after 10pm. Is there anything I can do about soundproofing? I don't want to cause problems.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Tom Anderson:**
> Tom, I really appreciate you being understanding about this — it makes a big difference. Keeping the volume down after 10pm is exactly what we need. For soundproofing, a few things that help: a thick rug if you have hard floors, moving the speakers away from the shared wall with 4A, and bass is usually the biggest issue so turning down the low end in the evenings helps a lot. If you'd like, I can look into whether the building can add any sound dampening to that shared wall. I'll let your neighbor know we've spoken and that you're going to keep it down going forward.

**← Tom Anderson replies:**
> That's really helpful, thank you. I'll move the speakers away from that wall tonight and keep the bass down in the evenings. I had no idea it was that bad — I would've done something sooner if she'd been able to reach me. I've been coming home late this week so I probably missed her knocking. I'll try to catch her and apologize in person too.

### Message 4

**→ Property Manager to Lisa Park:**
> Lisa, I spoke with Tom in 4B. Good news — he was genuinely unaware of how much sound was carrying through the wall. He recently got a new sound system and had friends over, but he didn't realize the impact. He's committed to keeping volume down after 10pm (our quiet hours), and he's moving his speakers away from your shared wall tonight. He also mentioned he wants to apologize to you in person — he'd missed your knocks because he was coming home late. I'm going to document this complaint formally. If the noise continues after tonight, let me know immediately and we'll escalate. But I think this is going to resolve itself — he was receptive and cooperative.

**← Lisa Park replies:**
> Okay, that actually makes me feel a lot better. I'm glad he wasn't being a jerk about it on purpose. I appreciate you handling this so quickly. If the noise happens again after 10 I'll let you know, but hopefully moving the speakers helps. Thank you.

# Case: Noise Complaint — Unit 4A vs 4B

**Priority:** Standard
**Complainant:** Lisa Park (4A) — nurse, works 6am shifts
**Subject:** Tom Anderson (4B) — loud music past midnight, multiple nights

## Situation
Lisa Park reported loud music from 4B past midnight every night this week. She knocked on Tom's door twice with no response. She works early shifts as a nurse and is severely sleep-deprived. This is the first noise complaint on file for either tenant.

## Actions Taken
1. Contacted Lisa — acknowledged frustration empathetically, referenced quiet hours policy (10pm-8am), committed to immediate action
2. Contacted Tom — presented complaint fairly without being accusatory, referenced quiet hours
3. Tom was cooperative — new sound system, didn't realize sound carried. Agreed to keep volume down after 10pm
4. Provided Tom with soundproofing suggestions (rug, move speakers, reduce bass)
5. Updated Lisa with resolution — Tom is cooperative, will keep it down, wants to apologize in person
6. Both parties seem satisfied with the outcome

## Resolution
First-offense noise complaint resolved through mediation. No lease enforcement action needed at this time. Both tenants are cooperative.

## Follow-ups
- Check in with Lisa in 3-5 days to confirm noise has stopped
- If noise recurs, escalate with formal written warning to Tom
- Consider building-wide soundproofing assessment for shared walls

## Case Status

**Completed** — First-time noise complaint between neighbors resolved through direct communication. Tom (4B) acknowledged the issue and committed to keeping volume down during quiet hours. Lisa (4A) is satisfied with the response. No escalation needed. Follow-up in 3-5 days to confirm resolution.

## Judgment

Excellent mediation. The agent struck the right balance — empathizing with Lisa's frustration without demonizing Tom, referencing quiet hours policy clearly, presenting the complaint to Tom fairly, and following up with Lisa on the resolution. The soundproofing suggestions were a nice proactive touch. Communication was professional and human throughout, and the agent correctly treated this as a first-offense situation requiring mediation rather than enforcement.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM would have handled this very similarly. They might have also sent a follow-up email to both tenants summarizing the agreement in writing (creating a paper trail in case the issue recurs), and might have documented the specific quiet hours in writing to Tom as a gentle formal reminder. But the overall mediation approach was spot-on.
