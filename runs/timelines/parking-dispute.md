# Unauthorized car in assigned spot

**Case ID:** parking-dispute
**Score:** 8/10

## Incoming Situation

Mike Williams (Unit 3B) is furious — someone has been parking in his assigned parking spot (#7) for the third time this week. He doesn't recognize the car. He needs to park NOW and has groceries in the car.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Mike Williams:**
> Mike, I completely understand your frustration — spot #7 is yours and someone parking there repeatedly is not okay. Let's solve the immediate problem first: can you park in one of the visitor spots for now so you can get your groceries inside? They're not ideal but it'll get you off the street. While you do that, I'm going to work on figuring out who this car belongs to and make sure it doesn't happen again. Can you tell me the make, model, and color of the car, and if you can see a license plate number?

**← Mike Williams replies:**
> Fine, I'll use a visitor spot but those are way at the end of the lot. The car is a silver Honda Civic, no idea on the plate — it was dark and I had groceries to deal with. This is the THIRD time this week. I want this actually resolved, not just a temporary fix. Can you have it towed?

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to Mike Williams:**
> I hear you — three times in a week is a pattern, not a one-off, and it needs a permanent solution. Here's what I'm going to do: first, I'll check with the other tenants to see if anyone recognizes the car or has a guest who might be using the lot. Second, I'm going to leave a warning notice on the car stating that this is a private assigned spot and unauthorized vehicles will be towed. Third, I'm going to talk to the owner about getting proper towing signage posted at the lot entrance so we have the authority to enforce towing going forward. If the car shows up in your spot again after the warning, we'll have it towed. Does that work?

**← Mike Williams replies:**
> Yes, that's more like it. I appreciate you actually doing something about it. The towing sign is a good idea — that should scare off whoever's doing this. Let me know what you find out about the car. And if it happens one more time I want it towed, no warnings.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Tom Anderson:**
> Hi Tom, quick question — have you or any guests been using parking spot #7 in the lot this week? We've had reports of an unrecognized silver Honda Civic parking there. Spot #7 is assigned to another tenant and it's happened three times. Just checking with all residents to see if anyone knows whose car it is.

**← Tom Anderson replies:**
> No, that's not me or anyone I know. My car is a black Toyota and I always park in my assigned spot. Maybe it's someone from the next building over? The lots are pretty close together and there's no signage.

### Message 4

**→ Property Manager to David Kim:**
> Hi David, quick question — do you know anyone with a silver Honda Civic who might be parking in our lot? We've had an unrecognized car using an assigned tenant spot multiple times this week. Checking with residents to track it down.

**← David Kim replies:**
> Hmm, I think my friend who visited last weekend drives a silver Civic, but I told her to use the visitor spots. I can ask her if she's been coming by without telling me? She lives nearby.

### Message 5

**→ Property Manager to David Kim:**
> That could be it — would you mind checking with her? If she has been using the lot, please let her know that the numbered spots are assigned to tenants and she'd need to use the visitor spots (they're marked). We're going to be posting towing signage soon, so anyone in the wrong spot could get towed. I'd hate for your friend to get a surprise tow. If it's not her, no worries — we'll keep investigating.

**← David Kim replies:**
> I just texted her and she admitted she's been parking there when she comes to the gym nearby. She didn't realize the spots were assigned — she said there's no signage. She's really sorry and says she won't do it again. I apologize on her behalf.

### Message 6

**→ Property Manager to Mike Williams:**
> Mike, mystery solved. The silver Civic belongs to a friend of one of our residents who was using the lot to park for a nearby gym. She didn't realize the spots were assigned — there's no signage, which is part of the problem. She's been told to stop and won't be using the lot again. I'm still going to follow through on the towing signage at the lot entrance so this can't happen with anyone else. I'm sorry for the hassle this week — your spot should be clear going forward.

**← Mike Williams replies:**
> Well at least it's resolved. Yeah, get that signage up so this doesn't happen again. Thanks for actually tracking it down — I appreciate you not just blowing me off.

### Message 7

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, quick update on a parking issue. Mike Williams (3B) had his assigned spot (#7) used by an unauthorized vehicle three times this week. I tracked it down — it was a friend of one of our tenants who was parking there for a nearby gym, didn't realize spots were assigned. She's been told to stop. The root cause is that we don't have towing signage at the lot entrance. I'd like to get a proper "Assigned Parking — Unauthorized Vehicles Will Be Towed" sign posted. These run about $30-50. It prevents future issues and gives us enforcement authority. Want me to go ahead and order one?

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> Yes, order the sign. $30-50 is nothing compared to dealing with angry tenants. Good detective work tracking down the car. Make sure all tenants know their spot numbers too while you're at it.

**Priority:** Standard (but tenant is very frustrated)

## Situation
Mike Williams (3B) reported unauthorized silver Honda Civic in his assigned spot #7 three times this week. Tenant is furious and has groceries to unload.

- Checked with Tom Anderson (4B) — not his car, suggested it might be from neighboring building
- Checked with David Kim (5B) — identified car as belonging to his friend who parks there for nearby gym

- Friend has been told to stop and apologized

## Actions Taken
1. Directed Mike to visitor spot for immediate parking
2. Committed to investigating and implementing permanent fix
3. Checked with multiple tenants — identified car owner through David Kim

5. Updated Mike with resolution

## Follow-ups
- Order and install towing signage at lot entrance

## Case Status

**Completed** — Unauthorized car identified and stopped. Root cause was lack of towing signage. Owner approved signage purchase. Mike informed of resolution and permanent fix. Follow-up: install signage and send building-wide parking reminder.

## Judgment

Strong handling of a frustrating situation. The agent immediately addressed Mike's urgent need (suggesting visitor spot), then systematically investigated the source by checking with tenants, identified the car through David Kim, resolved the immediate issue, and proposed a structural fix (towing signage) to prevent recurrence. Communication was empathetic with Mike and diplomatic with David. The only gap was not proactively leaving a note on the car as an additional measure.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM might have gone to the parking lot in person to photograph the car and leave a note immediately, rather than relying solely on tenant outreach. They also might have checked with the neighboring building's management since the lots are close together. But the systematic investigation and structural fix (signage) was a professional approach that goes beyond just resolving the immediate complaint.

### Behaviors Met

- Acknowledge Mike's frustration — this is his assigned spot
- Help with the immediate problem: suggest the visitor spot temporarily
- Try to identify the car owner — check with other tenants or leave a note on the car
- Warn that unauthorized vehicles may be towed
- If it's a repeat issue, consider posting towing signage
- Document the incidents in case notes

### Behaviors Missed
