# Cockroach sighting in Unit 5A

**Case ID:** pest-report
**Score:** 8/10

## Incoming Situation

Rachel Green (Unit 5A) reports seeing cockroaches in her kitchen — she says she's seen 3-4 over the past week, mostly at night near the sink.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Rachel Green:**
> Rachel, thank you for reporting this — I know seeing cockroaches is really unpleasant and I want to get this taken care of for you. A few quick questions: are you seeing them only near the kitchen sink, or anywhere else in the apartment? And are they small (under half an inch) or larger? This will help the exterminator know what they're dealing with. In the meantime, I'm going to contact our pest control company to schedule a treatment. The building covers the cost — this is the landlord's responsibility.

**← Rachel Green replies:**
> They're only in the kitchen, mostly near the sink and under the cabinets. They're about an inch long — definitely full-sized cockroaches, not tiny ones. I've seen 3-4 over the past week, always at night. And just so we're clear — I keep my apartment very clean. This is not a cleanliness issue on my end.

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to Rachel Green:**
> Absolutely — I'm not suggesting it's a cleanliness issue at all, Rachel. In multi-unit buildings, cockroaches often travel through shared plumbing and wall cavities regardless of how clean individual units are. That's actually why we'll want to check adjacent units too, to make sure we treat the source and not just the symptoms. A few things that can help in the meantime: make sure there's no standing water in the sink overnight (they're attracted to moisture), and store any open food in sealed containers. I'll have a date from pest control for you shortly.

**← Rachel Green replies:**
> Okay, thank you — I appreciate you not blaming me. I'll make sure there's no water sitting in the sink. Do you know if other units have reported this too? And when do you think pest control can come? I really don't want to keep seeing these things.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to BugFree Exterminators:**
> Hi, this is the property manager at 742 Evergreen Terrace. We have a cockroach report in Unit 5A — tenant has seen 3-4 full-sized cockroaches near the kitchen sink over the past week, mostly at night. This is a multi-unit residential building. I'd like to schedule treatment for 5A and also have adjacent units inspected. What's your earliest availability, and what's the cost per unit?

**← BugFree Exterminators replies:**
> For cockroaches in an apartment building, we recommend treating the affected unit and adjacent units. We can come in 2-3 days for a non-emergency appointment. Cost is $200 per unit treated. We'd recommend at least checking the units next to and below 5A. How many units would you like us to inspect?

### Message 4

**→ Property Manager to BugFree Exterminators:**
> Let's treat Unit 5A and inspect the adjacent units — 5B next door, and 4A below. If those inspections reveal activity, we'll treat them as well. Please schedule us for your earliest slot. The building address is 742 Evergreen Terrace. I'll make sure tenants are notified and units are accessible.

**← BugFree Exterminators replies:**
> We can schedule you for Thursday morning. We'll treat 5A and inspect 5B and 4A. If we find activity in those units, we can treat them the same day. Total for treatment of 5A plus inspections is $200 for the treatment, inspections are free. Additional treatments are $200 per unit if needed.

### Message 5

**→ Property Manager to Rachel Green:**
> Rachel, pest control is scheduled for Thursday morning. BugFree Exterminators will treat your unit and also inspect the adjacent units to make sure we're addressing the source. The building is covering all costs. You'll need to have your kitchen cabinets accessible — they may ask you to clear items from under the sink. They'll give you specific prep instructions when they confirm. Is Thursday morning okay for you to have someone in the unit?

**← Rachel Green replies:**
> Thursday works. I'll clear out under the sink and the kitchen cabinets. Thank you for handling this so quickly and for checking the other units too — that makes me feel like you're actually solving the problem, not just putting a bandaid on it.

## Judgment

The agent handled this well. It took the report seriously, was careful not to imply a cleanliness issue (which the tenant was sensitive about), provided practical prevention advice, scheduled pest control promptly, and correctly identified the need to check adjacent units in a multi-unit building. Owner was notified with clear cost information. The only gap was not proactively reaching out to adjacent tenants to ask if they'd noticed anything.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM would have also contacted the adjacent tenants (5B and 4A) directly to ask if they've seen any pest activity, rather than waiting for the exterminator to inspect. This would have given an earlier sense of scope. They also might have checked whether there were any prior pest reports in the building's history. But the core handling — prompt scheduling, multi-unit approach, cost transparency with owner — was solid.
