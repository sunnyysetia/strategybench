# Tenant wants to sublease

**Case ID:** sublease-request
**Score:** 9/10

## Incoming Situation

David Kim (Unit 5B) emails: 'I got a 6-month work assignment abroad starting next month. I'd like to sublease my apartment to a friend rather than break my lease. My friend is reliable — she's a nurse at the local hospital.'

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to David Kim:**
> David, congratulations on the work assignment — that sounds like a great opportunity. I'm happy to help you figure out the sublease. Here's how the process works: our lease requires written approval from the property owner for any sublease, and your friend would need to submit a standard rental application (credit check, background check, income verification). This is to protect you as much as anyone — you'd remain on the lease and be ultimately responsible for the unit during the sublease period. Can you have your friend start on the application? I'll send her the details once you give me her contact info. And to set expectations: approval typically takes about a week once we have the application in.

**← David Kim replies:**
> That all makes sense. Her name is Amy Chen — she's a registered nurse at St. Mary's Hospital, very responsible. She's excited about the apartment. Can you send me the application form so I can pass it to her? I leave in 4 weeks so I'd like to get this moving. Also, is there any additional deposit or fee for the sublease?

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to David Kim:**
> Great, I'll get the application details to you today so Amy can start right away. Regarding fees: there's no additional deposit required since your security deposit stays on the unit, but Amy would need renter's insurance for the sublease period. There's also no sublease fee. One important thing: you'll want to sign a sublease agreement directly with Amy that covers the 6-month period, rent payment expectations, and unit condition. I can provide a template for that. Let me check with the owner now on the sublease approval and I'll get back to you with everything you need.

**← David Kim replies:**
> Perfect, that's really helpful. A template would be great — I want to make sure everything is done properly. Amy is ready to fill out whatever's needed. Thanks for being so organized about this.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, David Kim in Unit 5B has requested to sublease his apartment for 6 months while he's on a work assignment abroad. He wants to sublease to a friend named Amy Chen, who he says is a registered nurse at St. Mary's Hospital. Per the lease, subleases require your written approval. I've told David that Amy needs to submit a standard rental application — credit check, background check, income verification. David would remain on the lease and be ultimately responsible. Are you open to considering the sublease once we have Amy's application? David leaves in 4 weeks, so there's some time pressure.

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> I'm open to subleases if the sublessee passes screening. Have Amy submit the application and run the standard checks. I want David to remain on the lease and be responsible for any damage or missed rent. Make sure there's a proper sublease agreement in place. Once screening clears, I'll approve it in writing.

### Message 4

**→ Property Manager to David Kim:**
> David, good news — the owner is open to the sublease pending Amy's application. Here's what Amy needs to submit: standard rental application with proof of income (pay stubs or employment letter), consent for credit and background check, and proof of renter's insurance. Once we have everything, screening takes about 5 business days. After that, the owner will provide written approval and we'll execute the sublease agreement. I'll put together the sublease template and send it along with the application. Can you get Amy started on this by end of the week so we stay on track with your timeline?

**← David Kim replies:**
> Absolutely. I'll talk to Amy tonight and we'll have the application done by Friday. She's got great credit and a stable job so the screening should be straightforward. Thank you for making this process so smooth — I was really stressed about what to do with the apartment and this takes a huge weight off.

# Case: Sublease Request — Unit 5B (David Kim)

**Priority:** Standard (but time-sensitive — tenant leaves in 4 weeks)

## Situation
David Kim (5B) received 6-month work assignment abroad. Wants to sublease to friend Amy Chen (registered nurse at St. Mary's Hospital). 14 months remaining on lease.

## Process
1. Sublease requires owner written approval (per lease)
2. Sublessee (Amy) must submit standard rental application
3. Screening: credit check, background check, income verification
4. David remains on lease — ultimately responsible
5. Sublease agreement needed between David and Amy

## Actions Taken
1. Explained sublease process to David — application, screening, approval timeline
2. Clarified: no additional deposit, no sublease fee, renter's insurance required
3. Consulted owner — Robert Hughes is open to sublease pending screening
4. Provided timeline: application by Friday, 5 business days for screening, then written approval
5. Offered sublease agreement template

## Timeline
- Week 1: Amy submits application
- Week 2: Screening (credit, background, income)
- Week 3: Owner written approval + sublease agreement signed
- Week 4: David departs, Amy moves in

## Follow-ups
- Send application and sublease template to David
- Process Amy's application once received
- Run screening (credit, background, income)
- Get written approval from Robert Hughes
- Execute sublease agreement
- Schedule key handoff and unit walkthrough

## Case Status

**Completed** — Sublease request processed. Owner approved pending screening. Application and sublease template being sent to David. Amy to submit application by Friday. Timeline on track for David's departure in 4 weeks. David remains on lease as ultimately responsible party.

## Judgment

The agent handled the sublease request very well. It explained the process clearly without approving or denying on the spot, correctly identified the need for owner approval and tenant screening, kept David on the lease as responsible party, and consulted the owner with a clear summary. The timeline management was proactive — working backwards from the 4-week departure to set deadlines. Communication was professional and organized.

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM would have handled this very similarly. They might have also asked David about his plans for mail forwarding, whether Amy would need parking, and who to contact in case of emergency at the unit while David is abroad. These operational details would have made the handoff smoother. But the process discipline — application, screening, owner approval, sublease agreement — was textbook.

### Behaviors Met

- Acknowledge David's request and explain the sublease approval process
- State that subleases require owner approval and the sublessee must apply
- Ask David's friend to submit a standard rental application
- Contact the owner to discuss the sublease request
- Don't approve or deny on the spot — follow proper process
- Document the request and timeline in case notes
