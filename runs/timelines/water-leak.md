# Ceiling leak in 4B

**Case ID:** water-leak
**Score:** 9/10

## Incoming Situation

Tom Anderson (Unit 4B) calls in a panic: water is dripping from his ceiling and it's getting worse. He says it started about an hour ago. A puddle is forming on the living room floor.

## Communication Timeline

### Message 1

**→ Property Manager to Tom Anderson:**
> Tom, I understand this is stressful — a ceiling leak is serious and I'm on it right now. First things first: please place towels or buckets under the drip and move any electronics or valuables away from the area. I'm going to contact the tenant above you and a plumber immediately. I'll keep you updated every step of the way.

**← Tom Anderson replies:**
> Thank you for getting back to me so quickly! I'll grab some towels and move my laptop and speakers right now. I've noticed the dripping is coming from near the light fixture which is making me nervous. Please hurry with the plumber!

### Message 2

**→ Property Manager to David Kim:**
> Hi David, this is your property manager. We have a water leak reported in the unit directly below yours (4B) — water is dripping from their ceiling. Could you check your apartment for any water on the floor, especially in the kitchen, bathroom, and laundry area? This is urgent — please check as soon as possible.

**← David Kim replies:**
> Oh no, I just checked and you're right — my washing machine hose has come loose and there's water pooling on my laundry room floor. I'm so sorry about this! I'll shut off the water to the machine right now.

### Message 3

**→ Property Manager to David Kim:**
> Thank you for checking so quickly, David. Please shut off the water to the washing machine immediately — that should stop the leak below. Don't worry, these things happen. I'm sending a plumber to check both units and make sure everything is properly reconnected. I'll follow up with timing.

**← David Kim replies:**
> I've shut it off completely. Again, I'm really sorry — I had no idea the hose was loose. Let me know when the plumber is coming and I'll make sure I'm here.

### Message 4

**→ Property Manager to Quick Fix Plumbing:**
> Hi, this is the property manager at 742 Evergreen Terrace. We have an active water leak — a washing machine hose came loose in Unit 5B, causing water damage in Unit 4B below. The water source has been shut off but we need someone to inspect both units, reconnect the hose properly, and assess any water damage to the ceiling in 4B. Can you send someone as soon as possible?

**← Quick Fix Plumbing replies:**
> We can have someone there within 45 minutes. We'll need access to both units. Emergency rate is $150/hour. We'll inspect the hose connection in 5B and check the ceiling in 4B for any damage to the drywall or fixtures.

### Message 5

**→ Property Manager to Tom Anderson:**
> Good news, Tom — we found the source. Your upstairs neighbor's washing machine hose had come loose. He's shut off the water, so the dripping should slow down and stop. I've called Quick Fix Plumbing and they'll be at your unit within 45 minutes to inspect the ceiling and make sure there's no damage to the drywall or light fixture. Is the dripping slowing down at all?

**← Tom Anderson replies:**
> Oh thank god, yes it's already slowing down! That's such a relief. I moved everything important away from the area. I'll be here when the plumber arrives. Thank you for handling this so fast — I was really panicking.

### Message 6

**→ Property Manager to Robert Hughes:**
> Robert, wanted to give you a heads up on a situation today. Water leak in Unit 4B caused by a loose washing machine hose in Unit 5B above. Source has been identified and shut off. Quick Fix Plumbing is en route (ETA 45 min, $150/hr emergency rate) to inspect both units. The tenant in 4B reported water near a light fixture, so we may have some drywall damage to assess. I'll send you the plumber's full report once they've completed the inspection. No injuries, both tenants are cooperating.

**← Robert Hughes replies:**
> Thanks for the quick response. Good that you found the source fast. Let me know what the plumber says about the ceiling damage — if it's significant we may need to file an insurance claim. Also make sure the washing machine connection in 5B is properly secured so this doesn't happen again.

### Message 7

**→ Property Manager to Joe's Maintenance:**
> Hi Joe, heads up — we had a water leak in Unit 4B today (washing machine hose issue from 5B above). Quick Fix Plumbing is handling the plumbing inspection now. Once they're done, I may need you to come assess and repair any drywall damage in 4B's ceiling, particularly near a light fixture. I'll follow up once I have the plumber's report. No rush, but wanted you in the loop.

**← Joe's Maintenance replies:**
> Thanks for the heads up! I can come by tomorrow or the day after to look at the drywall once the area has dried out. Ceiling patches near light fixtures are pretty standard — usually $75-100 depending on the size. Just let me know when the plumber clears it.

## Judgment

The agent handled this emergency situation very well. It correctly triaged the issue as urgent, immediately contacted the affected tenant with practical advice, investigated the source by contacting the upstairs neighbor, dispatched a plumber, and notified both the property owner and maintenance. Communication was empathetic and specific throughout, with clear next steps provided to all parties. The only minor gap was not advising Tom about the light fixture safety concern (water near electrical fixtures).

### How a Human PM Would Differ

An experienced human PM would have handled this very similarly. They might have additionally warned Tom about the safety risk of water near the light fixture (advising him to avoid turning it on until inspected) and would have taken photos for documentation. They also might have proactively mentioned the insurance claim possibility to the owner rather than waiting for the plumber's report.

- Tell Tom to place towels/buckets and move valuables away from the leak
